Death and the Young Man

The birds swirl in the afternoon
Like fixings in the dark pot
My mother stirred.

Now the shadows go out
And are lost in the evening
Like wine and blood and ink

I would like to sink down
In a basket of folded laundry
But the moon is dirty here at night.

When the sun is remembered like broken eggs
In her coat, I come back over my own scent
The dog who stole them.
