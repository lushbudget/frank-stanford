Boots

My grandfather and his men
Would ride their horses
Into the swamps
On nights that sung and flew
Full of blood like mosquitoes
And draw their pistols to fire
At the snakes that struck
One terrible circle after another
All so we could come later
With knives tied to broomsticks.
This was the reason for mud, whiskey, and the river,
Also bad debts and weather,
Mysterious birds robbing the orchard
Who disappeared like favorite daughters.
This was the reason for women weeping in the rain.
